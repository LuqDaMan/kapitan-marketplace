# Hedge Fund (Multi-Manager) Profile

## Representative Firms
Balyasny, Citadel, Point72, Millennium, ExodusPoint, Schonfeld

## Business Model
- Multi-manager pod structure: independent PMs with allocated capital
- Gross/net exposure limits per pod
- Central risk management oversight
- High turnover, short-to-medium term horizon
- Performance fees: 20%+ of profits, high-water mark

## What They Test In Interviews
- Sharp market views with conviction
- Quick cross-asset thinking
- Risk management discipline
- Quantitative reasoning and probability
- Ability to generate alpha, not just beta
- Trade idea generation and pitch quality
- Mental math and brain teasers

## Interview Emphasis by Section (M1-M5)
- M1 (Resume): 10% — brief, focus on PnL mindset, prior trading/research experience
- M2 (Market Views): 25% — must have sharp, contrarian views. "What's your best idea?" is key
- M3 (Trade Pitch): 30% — THE most important section. Must be specific: entry, exit, sizing, stop loss
- M4 (Scenario): 25% — fast, structured cross-asset thinking. Second-order effects expected
- M5 (Brain Teaser): 10% — probability, expected value, mental math

## Culture & Values
- Meritocratic, performance-driven
- Fast-paced, high-pressure
- Intellectual curiosity rewarded
- Risk management is paramount — "don't blow up"
- Collaboration within pods, competition between pods

## What Makes a Candidate Stand Out
- Original trade ideas (not consensus)
- Quantified risk/reward on every pitch
- Deep understanding of a specific niche
- Comfort with being wrong — but knowing when to cut

## Common Mistakes
- Giving generic macro views without trade expression
- Not sizing positions
- Ignoring risk management
- Being too academic — they want practical insight
