# Asset Manager Profile

## Representative Firms
BlackRock, PIMCO, Fidelity, Vanguard, Capital Group, Wellington, T. Rowe Price, Bridgewater

## Business Model
- Long-only mandates: manage assets on behalf of institutional/retail clients
- AUM-based fees: typically 0.1-1% of AUM
- Benchmark-relative performance (vs S&P 500, Bloomberg Agg, etc.)
- Investment styles: active vs passive, fundamental vs quantitative
- Product types: mutual funds, ETFs, separate accounts, alternatives

## What They Test In Interviews
- Investment philosophy and process
- Fundamental analysis skills
- Long-term thinking (months to years horizon)
- Portfolio construction knowledge
- Understanding of benchmarks and tracking error
- Macroeconomic awareness
- Stock/sector/asset class pitches with fundamental thesis

## Interview Emphasis by Section (M1-M5)
- M1 (Resume): 15% — investment philosophy, why asset management vs trading
- M2 (Market Views): 20% — longer-term thematic views, big picture macro
- M3 (Trade Pitch): 30% — fundamental thesis, valuation work, longer time horizon
- M4 (Scenario): 20% — portfolio impact, rebalancing decisions
- M5 (Brain Teaser): 15% — market sizing, back-of-envelope valuation

## Culture & Values
- Intellectual rigor, research-oriented
- Longer-term perspective
- Collaborative investment process
- Client fiduciary duty paramount
- Work-life balance generally better than banks/HFs

## What Makes a Candidate Stand Out
- Deep fundamental analysis on a specific stock/sector
- Clear investment philosophy articulation
- Understanding of portfolio construction (not just stock picking)
- Awareness of ESG/sustainable investing trends
- Knowledge of passive vs active debate

## Common Mistakes
- Pitching a short-term trade (they want long-term theses)
- Not understanding benchmark-relative performance
- Ignoring portfolio construction / correlation
- Being too focused on trading vs investing
