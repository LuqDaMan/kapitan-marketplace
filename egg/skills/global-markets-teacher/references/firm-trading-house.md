# Commodity Trading House Profile

## Representative Firms
Glencore, Trafigura, Vitol, Gunvor, Mercuria, Koch Industries, Louis Dreyfus, Cargill

## Business Model
- Physical commodity trading: buy, transport, store, blend, deliver physical commodities
- Arbitrage: geographic, temporal, quality
- Asset ownership: mines, refineries, storage, shipping
- Vertically integrated supply chains
- Paper trading to hedge physical exposure
- Revenue: spread capture (not directional bets), logistics optimization

## What They Test In Interviews
- Understanding of physical commodity markets
- Supply chain and logistics knowledge
- Commercial instinct: where are the margins?
- Geographical and geopolitical awareness
- Quantitative skills (moderate)
- Understanding of basis, contango/backwardation, physical arbitrage
- Comfort with Incoterms, shipping, storage

## Interview Emphasis by Section (M1-M5)
- M1 (Resume): 15% — why commodities, commercial mindset, geographic mobility
- M2 (Market Views): 20% — commodity-specific supply/demand analysis
- M3 (Trade Pitch): 25% — physical trade with logistics angle, not just paper
- M4 (Scenario): 25% — physical flow impact, supply chain disruption analysis
- M5 (Brain Teaser): 15% — logistics estimation, volume/tonnage calculations

## Culture & Values
- Entrepreneurial, deal-making culture
- Geographic mobility (often relocate to Geneva, Singapore, Houston)
- Results-oriented, less hierarchical
- Long hours during deal execution
- Traders have P&L responsibility early
- Relationship-driven (with producers, refiners, shippers)

## What Makes a Candidate Stand Out
- Understanding of physical commodity value chain
- Knowledge of specific commodity markets (oil, metals, ags)
- Commercial instinct — identifying arbitrage opportunities
- Willingness to be hands-on (visit mines, ports, refineries)
- Language skills and cultural awareness

## Common Mistakes
- Treating commodity trading like financial trading
- Not understanding physical delivery mechanics
- Ignoring logistics and supply chain aspects
- Not knowing Incoterms
- Being too focused on paper/derivatives
